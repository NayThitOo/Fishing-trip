Welcome to Myanmar's waterways

Myanmar is home to rich rivers, lakes and coastal waters where fishing is both livelihood and culture. Below you'll find photos, a short video, and an audio track that evokes the river life.

Tip: click the links in the navigation to see two more pages with more photos and local tips.

Quick facts

- Primary fishing areas: Irrawaddy River, Inle Lake, coastal zones.
- Popular catches: carp, catfish, tilapia and local freshwater species.
- Best months: generally cooler, drier months for comfortable trips.

Remember to respect local rules and practice sustainable fishing.

How to get involved

If you want to experience fishing in Myanmar: join a local guide or community-run tour. Many villages offer catch-and-cook experiences and short boat trips.

Why fish in Myanmar?

Myanmar's rivers and lakes offer a mix of scenic beauty and abundant fish species. Local fishing supports many families and communities; when visiting, choose responsible operators.
